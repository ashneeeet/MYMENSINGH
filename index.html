Mymensingh's Hidden Gems

HISTORY

Mymensingh, a historic city in Bangladesh, was established as a district in 1787 during British rule. Known for its rich cultural heritage, it served as an administrative and educational hub. The region thrived on jute cultivation and trade, earning fame as the "Golden Fiber City." It houses institutions like Mymensingh Medical College.
